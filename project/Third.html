National Flag Of Nepal


The national flag of Nepal is one of the most distinctive flags in the world, as it is the only non-rectangular national flag. Its design consists of two crimson red triangular pennants stacked vertically, bordered in blue. The upper triangle bears a white crescent moon with eight rays, while the lower triangle features a white twelve-rayed sun. The crimson red symbolizes bravery and the color of the rhododendron, Nepal’s national flower, while the blue border represents peace. The moon and sun are traditional symbols of permanence, reflecting Nepal’s hope that the nation will last as long as these celestial bodies. Officially adopted on December 16, 1962, the flag’s unique double-pennon shape sets Nepal apart from all other countries in terms of national symbolism.
